Case Study - Improving the Social Feature of the Nike Run Club

For this case study, I took the role of a product manager for Nike Run Club, Nike’s running tracker and training program application. To arrive at well-developed a solution, I followed a rigorous product management process by conducting user research, identifying pain points, applying PM-specific frameworks to narrow down a viable opportunity and designing a solution.

Deliverables:

  1. User Personas

  2. Opportunity Map

  3. Feature Prioritization

  4. Product Requirement Document

  5. Visual Mockups (Low-fi and High-fi)

Introducing Nike Run Club

Growing up in Vancouver, fitness and health has always been an integral part of my life. Surrounded by beautiful mountains and living by the coast, I found myself partaking in a lot of outdoor activities and specifically running when I entered university. This summer, I decided to challenge my running abilities by setting a goal of completing a half marathon by the end of the summer. While I was training independently for the first few weeks, I quickly found myself training with the Nike Run Club app and it slowly became one of my favourite trackers for running. 

From distance, elevation and pace tracking to guided run and training plans, the mobile application had all the features to be considered an all-in-one tracker. Users on the app are able to record their outdoor or treadmill runs while keeping a history of past activities. Coupled with a simple user interface (UI), the app felt seamless where pages and features were easy to use and quick to navigate. 

Project Objective

Situation:

During my time using the application, there were pain points I have identified as well as features that I would want to see within the application. 

1. Community. As a runner, there’s never a better feeling than running with your running partner, group, or friends where you feel challenged and supported throughout the entire run. This sense of community and relationship you build through these activities is one of the biggest reasons why I personally run and have stuck by this hobby to this day. While the Nike Run Club has a leaderboard and a friend request feature, it lacks interaction between users and the social aspect of running.

2. Prognostic and Diagnostic with Running Data. Another big reason why I love fitness tracking applications is because of the data you can collect and the ability to observe your progress through quantitative results. With today’s focus on data, there are a plethora of parameters collected during each run that can be used to build a better understanding of the body, the progress of the runner, and trends. 

Assumption:

  • Runners feel more encouraged and supported when they are socially connected with other runners

  • The data that is collected from runs is a big reason users rely on tracking apps to determine their progress

  • The Nike Run Club app provides guidance and structure to runners through training plans and guided runs

Hypothesis: 

  • The Nike Run Club can build community through a more socially transparent and user-responsive feature

  • The addition of data-driven features will give users a more comprehensive and in-depth analysis of their training

User Research

For user research, I surveyed five existing users of the application and relied on sources comprised of App Store reviews, running blogs, and subreddits (r/running). Many of the pain points expressed by reviews were centred around the social aspect of the application and the lack of engagement between users. These themes were further explored through categorization in the user personas and an opportunity map

User Persona 

After compiling and distilling the insights from the user research phase, I realized the frustrations and improvements expressed by users are highly dependent on their goal in running. Those who are less driven in running seek structure within the application while those who are more self-disciplined tend to explore more social features. Solution

Potential Solutions

  1. Integration with social media platforms

    • Similar to provider authentication, users are given the ability to connect with other users through other services such as Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, etc.

  2. Sharing of user metadata (runs, shoes, location) with other users

    • The user's profile will display information they can already see to the public such as the shoes they use, individual running data, and events they have participated in (Users can choose the visibility of the metadata)

  3. Add weekly progress for training plans

    • On the training plan page, running data is distilled and segregated per week for users to see their progress

  4. Remove the leaderboard feature

    • At the moment, users can view other runners' progress by seeing how many kilometres they have left to catch up to another user. This will be removed as it promotes competitiveness and an unhealthy perception on the activity.

  5. Adding notifications to remind the runners

    • This is a support feature to remind users who are inactive or following a rigorous training plan to open the app and incentivize the activity of running.

Feature Prioritization

Value Proposition

Nike Run Club's implementation of sharing user metadata and integration with other social providers will enable users to feel more supported by a community of runners and increase accessibility in developing connections between users.

Design

Product Requirements

  1. Users must be able to connect with at least one provider when searching for friends

  2. Users can view another user’s running progress weekly, monthly, and annually

  3. Users can view the shoes, friends and clubs of other users

Design Requirements

  1. Users are able to quickly navigate between different time periods for running data

  2. Profiles should be visual and be quickly interpreted by users from a glance

  3. Integration with other social media providers should be visual and modular for the addition of onboarding more providers

  4. A graphical approach for running data will be more user-friendly for long periods

Conclusion

Risk and Mitigation

Privacy - With the implementation of sharing features, it comes naturally that privacy and user permission will be a potential risk for the application. Especially with runs that involve GPS and health information, it is important to be cognizant of sensitive information such as running metadata when enabling sharing between users. 

Looking Forward

Moving forward, I want to spend more time working on user research and developing a more fundamental understanding of the users and how to better support them.

If given more time, there are more frameworks I would like to explore and analyze to see if the outcome of this project would change. Additionally, I would love to build this application on React Native or Swift to see the development efforts and the design becoming reality.

Reflection and Learnings

After completing this project, it felt as if I took my first step into product management. From working with different frameworks to learning the processes used in the industry, it was a breath of fresh air to see a product I use every day through the lens of a product manager.

I learned the importance of taking a large number of pain points and really narrowing them down to one solution using tools and prioritization. Moreover, the notion of rephrasing problems and taking unorthodox approaches to problem-solving has changed my mindset when finding solutions.

Notability Kids

For this phase, I targeted a more niche section of students to focus on early childhood education.

Understanding Notability

  1. What do you think the company's vision is for its product?

    • Vision: be the go-to note-taking software for students of all ages

    • Notability wants to act as a pen and paper. Drawing, calendar making, marking, chart making, brainstorming, etc.

  2. What do you think the product's current goal/trajectory is?

    • Notability recently changed its pricing model to a subscription instead of a one-time payment to increase revenues

  3. What is the value that the product provides for its current segment(s)/market(s)?

    For mature students:

    • Intuitive, convenient, and useful

    • Makes remembering things easier and faster to take notes

    • Everything you need all in one place, organize your thoughts/notes/drawings/templates

Notability Kids Solution

Problem

Screen time for children has a bad reputation. Learning new things or exercising creativity on an iPad is not the same as watching TV for hours on end.

Based on brief Reddit research (R/Sciencebasedparenting, r/parenting, r/daddit) there is a common pain point:

  • Scared of excessive screentime related to Youtube, TV, games, etc as they think this extensive stimulation ruining their child's attention span --> time to introduce a gamified learning

  • Trying to limit screen time but the child absolutely loses it --> redefine screen time as a tool to learn

  • Constantly asking for education app recommendations --> unaware of the market

Solutions

  • Implement writing lesson templates (ex. tracing letters, sentence formation,

    • Gamify: Create an avatar profile and can win prizes with consistent practice

  • Add more art features for kids to be creative

    • Stickers, interactive/sound art, more drawing tips

  • Use the recording feature to create music + add fun sounds

  • Collaboration feature so they can draw with other kids

New Value Proposition: Notability Kids!

For kids, Notability is an effective way for children to learn how to read and write by having an interactive and gamified design, allowing them to have fun while learning.

Notability kids will be on the same app as Notability. They will be able to switch between the platforms in Settings.

User Stories

Pricing + Sales Strategy

Pricing

The pricing will consist of three tiers. Each will have a different amount of lesson plans, gamified features, etc.

Basic learner (Free)

Advanced learner (paid $)

Expert learner (paid $$)

Sales Strategy

Acquisition: Ads on other child apps (both educational and noneducational), social media + collaboration with parent channels (preferably new parents as they will be more likely to try)

Engagement: Challenges, and other gamified aspects

Retention/conversion: Once a learning tier is completed, we will encourage upgrading to the next one

Metrics

  • Customer acquisition cost

  • Retention rate

  • Churn rate

  • DAU

  • Daily time used on app

  • How many people pay to upgrade to the next learner level

Conclusion

Risks:

The market research I conducted was more toward Notability as a whole and not toward early childhood education apps. I didn't get to go super in-depth but from my research, I did find it is a large market with lots of room for growth. Many apps don't require a stylus however Notability Kids will prepare children for the real world and provides additional mobility skills.

Another risk would be related to children's short attention spans. Therefore, I want to implement daily challenges where you can earn points (stars), which can be used for prizes determined in the future.

Next steps

If I had more time, I would flesh out the daily challenge system. I would also implement customization for the tiger/avatar, where kids could interact with it a little more. I would also include a more detailed "report card view" in the parent's view. I would also flesh out the pricing more, using market research to determine this.

Learnings

I learned how to follow through with an idea, completely fail, pivot and then start again. This was one of the most valuable lessons for me as I got to really "think" like a PM instead of a student where you have all the instructions laid out for you!

Spotify Classroom

For this phase, I targeted a more niche segment of Spotify users to focus on music learning and teaching.

Understanding Spotify

  1. What do you think the company’s vision is for their product?

  • For music creators, Spotify aims to provide a platform where artists can showcase their creativity, grow a fanbase, inspire listeners, and make a living

  • For music listeners, Spotify aims to provide a platform where listeners can enjoy an immersive artistic experience, empathize with one another, and be part of a wider community

  1. What do you think the product’s current goal/trajectory is?

  • Expanding beyond the genre of music and podcasting to become the leading player where 50 million creators will be able to manage and monetize their business to more than one billion users

  • Make Spotify available to anyone on any device from cars to kitchen appliances so people can enjoy personalization and discoverability 

  1. What is the value that the product provides for its current segment(s)/market(s)?

  • Its intuitive design is easy to navigate and use for discovering new music and personalizing playlists with other users

  • Freemium model -  free version is a limited ad-supported service and the paid premium version offers unlimited access within the platform

Discovering New Markets

  1. Potential markets

  • Music Teachers

  • Live music creators

  • Readers 

  • Children

  • Therapy

  1. Market Opportunity Analysis

Opportunity

Music teachers who are associated with primary or secondary school, music institutions, or independent. 

Customers

  • Actively listening to music is a key component to learning music, students prefer Spotify because of its superior quality

  • Teachers put together playlists for students and can upload their own music 

  • Send home required listening for students before the next lesson

  • Spotify’s real time lyrics can be further modified to include real time notations for assistance with practicing

Internal/External Factor (PESTLE)

Political

Governments have been getting stricter on anti-piracy and copyright laws which helps to protect the digital music industry.

Economic

The looming economic recession may not be a deterrent as the lipstick index suggests that during times of financial hardship, people move their spending to “affordable luxuries”. 

Social

Pricing strategy is affordable and speaks to its “price-conscious” millennial users by providing more for less. It is a platform that can be localized (culture, language, style). 

Technology

87% of teenagers in Canada have cell phones. Young people are increasingly owning more technology and becoming digital consumers. Access to the internet is increasing globally. 

Legal

Regulations differ across jurisdictions. More amateur creators producing content as a hobby might require different legislation. User privacy and data collection. 

Environment

Rise of streaming services eliminates the need for physical products which use lesser natural resources. Spotify uses Google cloud servers which rely on renewable energy. Increased electronic devices lead to greater toxic waste.

Setting Objectives and Metrics

Objective

Create a space for music teachers so they can produce a future generation of artists for Spotify

Key Results

KR1 - Create a pilot with 100 music teachers from different backgrounds in North America within the next year

KR2 - Convert 20% of students from these classrooms into premium users

KR3 - Develop a new pricing model for music teachers and their student

KR4 - Create classroom feature in the next 4 months

Competitive Landscape

PESTLE ANALYSIS
Political

Governments have been getting stricter on anti-piracy and copyright laws which helps to protect the digital music industry.

Economic

The looming economic recession may not be a deterrent as the lipstick index suggests that during times of financial hardship, people move their spending to “affordable luxuries”. 

Social

Pricing strategy is affordable and speaks to its “price-conscious” millennial users by providing more for less. It is a platform that can be localized (culture, language, style). More and more education is moving to remote online platforms. 

Technology

87% of teenagers in Canada have cell phones. Young people are increasingly owning more technology and becoming digital consumers. Access to the internet is increasing globally. 

Legal

Regulations differ across jurisdictions. More amateur creators producing content as a hobby might require different legislation. User privacy and data collection. 

Environment

Rise of streaming services eliminates the need for physical products which use lesser natural resources. Spotify uses Google cloud servers which rely on renewable energy. Increased electronic devices lead to greater toxic waste.

Market Sizing (US): Edited

TAM - 31,862 music teachers currently employed in the United States, 955,860 music students (30 student per teacher)

SAM - 1,275 music teachers (4% of American pay for music streaming app, music teachers are the customers)

SOM - 15% of SAM can be obtained in the timeframe of a year through direct partnership with elementary and secondary school boards

Problem:

Spotify is widely used by people of all ages for the purpose of listening to music, even by music students. Listening to music is a vital component of learning music, and young students prefer Spotify because of its superior quality. Yet, this is only one part of learning music, other aspects of learning are scattered across different platforms. These include examination, tuners, instructions and many more. This creates a unique set of pain points for a specific demographic of users - music students and their teachers.

Solutions:

  • Classroom feature

    • Assigned listening

    • Announcements

    • Instrument tuner

    • Notation accompaniment 

    • Record and upload (exam)

    • AI feedback

Value Prop Statement:

Spotify’s classroom addition will help to aid its goal of giving a million artists a creative platform by addressing the stage where creators are made - in music classrooms. By making music education more dynamic and versatile, the Spotify ecosystem will empower a new generation of creators.

Go To Market Strategy:

Trying to reach (Pivot)

  • Rely on Spotify’s B2C market and market directly to consumers

  • In 5 years, partner with educational institutions

Pricing strategy

  • Monthly $60 subscription fee or annual pricing 

  • Students don’t need subscription

  • Students get special pricing for premium (currently only for post-secondary)

Marketing strategy:

  • Promotional material online

  • Face to face engagement with pilot schools

  • Giving back to music community

Metrics:

  • 500 Spotify Classroom subscriptions within the first 6 months

  • 70% of students (under 18) become premium users

  • 5% of students from Spotify Classrooms become creators in the next 10 years

Product Strategy:

Continuous value creation

  • Expand to the post secondary higher education market

  • Support graduates of Spotify Classroom in becoming creators on the platform

  • Offer special student pricing to Spotify Classroom students for Spotify Premium 

Differentiating features

  • Empowering music educators 

  • Tools for both teachers and students

  • Immersive music experience 

  • Value added subscription pricing

Pivot Plan (If issues arise with the introduction of the features)

  • Establish customer advisory board

  • Identify issue (product feature or pricing model or market segment)

  • Redo user research and solution ideation

  • Conduct testing of new modification and adapt

  • Go to market

Design:

This feature will be available with a different subscription pricing within the same platform. It can be accessed on desktop and mobile devices.

Conclusion:

Risks:

The population of professional music teachers and learners is quite small and is not growing. Given this small segment of people, attracting those who engage in music learning as a hobby will be crucial. Currently, platform users play a passive role and this addition requires active users. These are some of the potential risks facing Spotify. 

Another risk is the lack of video features. When learning music, video demonstrations are incredibly helpful which Spotify Classroom does not have.

Next steps:

There is a tremendous marketing opportunity in collaborating with top artists to offer master classes through Spotify Classroom. These can be made interactive with AI and visuals (i.e., videos and animations). Eventually, I would like to create the opportunity for learners to showcase their creation. 

Learnings:

Trying to break into a new market was difficult with a large platform like Spotify because they have expanded into many different opportunities to become an entire ecosystem. I had to think creatively and dive deeper into smaller subgroups of users to identify a niche pain point. When designing a solution, I had many different ideas and it was challenging to narrow them down but I found it helpful to think from a strategic point of view. By considering which solutions would have the most impact in the shortest time frame, I was able to stick to my roadmap. Lastly, I learned the importance of pivoting, even when at the very late stage of the project. After receiving feedback, I went back to the drawing board and changed parts of my project to better fit the problem I was trying to solve.
